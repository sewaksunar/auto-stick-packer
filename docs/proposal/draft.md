## Background Theory

The proposed machine is based on the principles of mechanical automation, where repetitive counting and bundling operations are performed using mechanical indexing and intermittent motion mechanisms. The machine consists of a stick feeding unit, a mechanical counting mechanism, a bundling unit, and a binding mechanism that operate in a synchronized sequence.

The theoretical foundation of the project includes:

* **Kinematics and Dynamics of Mechanisms:** Analysis of linkages, cams, ratchet-pawl systems, and intermittent motion mechanisms to achieve accurate stick counting and bundle transfer.
* **Mechanical Counting Principle:** Each incense stick advances a counting wheel or ratchet by one increment. After a predetermined number of sticks is reached, the mechanism mechanically triggers the bundling operation without electronic sensors.
* **Machine Element Design:** Design of shafts, bearings, springs, gears, and mechanical joints to ensure smooth and reliable operation.
* **Structural Mechanics and Material Selection:** Selection of suitable materials and structural components to withstand operational loads while maintaining durability and cost-effectiveness.
* **CAD Modeling and Simulation:** Use of engineering software for modeling, kinematic simulation, and design validation before fabrication.

## Problem Statement

The counting and bundling of incense sticks in many small and medium-scale industries are still performed manually, resulting in low productivity, inconsistent bundle sizes, labor fatigue, and increased production costs. Existing automated systems often rely on expensive electronic sensors and control systems that may not be affordable for local manufacturers.

There is a need for a simple, low-cost, and reliable semi-automatic machine capable of mechanically counting and binding incense sticks with high accuracy. The proposed project aims to develop a mechanical counting and bundling system that reduces manual effort, improves productivity, and provides consistent bundle quality.

---

## Literature Review

Previous studies on automated packaging and counting systems indicate that mechanical indexing mechanisms, ratchet-pawl systems, Geneva mechanisms, and cam-operated linkages are effective for repetitive industrial operations. These mechanisms provide accurate motion control while minimizing dependence on electronic components.

Research in machine design also highlights the importance of kinematic analysis, material selection, and structural optimization for developing reliable and economical semi-automatic machines. CAD-based modeling and simulation techniques have further improved the design and validation process.

However, there is limited research focused on the development of a low-cost, purely mechanical incense stick counting and binding machine suitable for small-scale industries. This project addresses that gap by integrating proven mechanical mechanisms into a practical and affordable solution.
